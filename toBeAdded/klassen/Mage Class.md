# Mage Class

*The standard path of all magically gifted citizens of the Griffon Kingdom. Mages channel ambient world energy through memorized runic patterns, hand signs, and verbal incantations. Their mana cores grow naturally with age and practice, peaking around their twentieth year. Most graduates of the White Griffon Academy remain Mages all their lives — talented, resourceful, and largely ignorant of the deeper secrets Mogar keeps buried.*

## Class Features

As a Mage, you gain the following class features.

### Hit Points

| **Hit Dice**            | 1d6 per Mage level                  |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| **HP at 1st Level**     | 6 + Constitution modifier                |
| **HP at Higher Levels** | 1d6 (or 4) + Con mod per level after 1st |

### Proficiencies

| **Armor**         | None                                                                                              |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Weapons**       | Simple weapons                                                                                    |
| **Tools**         | None                                                                                              |
| **Saving Throws** | Intelligence, Wisdom                                                                              |
| **Skills**        | Choose two from Arcana, History, Investigation, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Insight, Persuasion |

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following items, plus anything provided by your background:
- Academy-issued student robes
- A formula tome (your spellbook)
- A component pouch or arcane focus
- 10 gp monthly academy stipend (first month pre-paid)

Alternatively, you may roll for starting gold and purchase your own equipment.

## Cantrips (Tier 0)

You know three cantrips of your choice from the general magic list. Cantrips can be cast at will without expending mana dice, provided you have at least one mana die remaining in your pool.

**Cantrip Improvement.** At 4th level you learn one additional cantrip, and again at 10th level.

**The Ember Rule.** Cantrip effects are inherently low-magnitude. Attempting to stretch a cantrip beyond safety (increasing its damage die, range, duration, or replicating a higher-tier effect) requires an Arcana check (DC = 10 + the effective tier you aim for). Success bends the spell; failure wastes the action and may cause unpredictable backlash.

## Mana Dice Pool

Your mana pool stores, filters, and releases raw magical energy. You have a pool of mana dice as shown in The Mage Level Table. You spend dice to cast spells of Tier 1 and above.

**Recovery.** You regain all spent mana dice when you finish a long rest. You regain half your maximum dice (rounded up) when you finish a short rest.

**Casting a Tiered Spell.** When you cast a spell of Tier 1 or higher, you must:
1. **Declare** the spell and any **Enhancements** (spending extra dice to improve the spell).
2. **Spend dice:** Subtract the base cost (Tier 1 = 1 die, Tier 2 = 2 dice, Tier 3 = 3 dice, etc.) plus any enhancement dice from your pool.
3. **Roll** all the spent dice. The total is your **Power Pool**.
4. **Accuracy check:** Roll a d20 + your relevant ability modifier (usually Intelligence) versus the target's AC or the target's saving throw, depending on the spell's design.
5. **Allocate** the Power Pool between Accuracy and damage/effect (for damage spells, typically all dice go to damage after accuracy is met).
6. **Affinity Bonus:** Add bonus dice if the spell matches your elemental affinities (+1d6 for primary, +1d4 for secondary).

Full details of the spellcasting system are given in the campaign magic document.

## Formula Preparation

You keep a **formula tome** (spellbook) containing all the spell formulas you have ever studied. There is no limit to how many formulas your tome can hold.

When you finish a long rest you may prepare a list of spells from your tome that you can cast that day. The number of spells you can have prepared equals your **Intelligence modifier + your Mage level** (minimum one spell).

**Swapping Specialized Formulas.** If you have the relevant academic specialization, you can swap one prepared formula for another specialized formula during a short or long rest with 1 minute of concentration.

**Swapping Non-Specialized Formulas.** Swapping a formula for one outside your specialization takes 10 minutes of study per tier of the spell and can only be done during a long rest.

## Mage Foundation (Levels 1–3)

At 1st level you choose one of three foundational approaches to magic taught during your first three years at the White Griffon Academy. This choice reflects your natural fighting style and academic focus.

- **Pure Mage (Scholar)** — raw mana manipulation, spell breadth, and efficiency
- **Mage Knight (Vanguard)** — weaving magic into physical combat
- **Arcane Scout (Stalker)** — mobility, stealth, and honed magical senses

### Pure Mage (Scholar)

*You focus on raw mana manipulation, spell breadth, and efficiency.*

**Active Flow (1st level).** **Freischaltung:** Stufe 1. When you roll your mana dice for a spell, you can reroll a number of dice equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum 1). You must use the new rolls.

**Mana Cycle (2nd level).** **Freischaltung:** Stufe 2. Once per long rest, when you finish a short rest you can recover a number of expended mana dice equal to your Proficiency Bonus.

**Elemental Shift (3rd level).** **Freischaltung:** Stufe 3. When you cast a spell of an element you have an affinity for, you can expend 1 additional mana die to change its damage type to another element you have at least a secondary affinity for. This does not alter the spell's tier or area.

### Mage Knight (Vanguard)

*You weave magic into physical combat, reinforcing your body and weaponry.*

**Martial Training (1st level).** **Freischaltung:** Stufe 1. You gain proficiency with medium armor, shields, and martial weapons.

**Mana Ward (1st level).** **Freischaltung:** Stufe 1. When you take damage, you can use your reaction to expend up to 2 mana dice from your pool. Roll them and reduce the incoming damage by the total + your Constitution modifier.

**Spell Channeling (2nd level).** **Freischaltung:** Stufe 2. When you take the Attack action, you can expend a bonus action to infuse your weapon with a Tier 1 or Tier 2 spell. The next time you hit with that weapon before the end of your turn, the spell discharges against the struck creature. (The spell must have a range of Touch or require a melee spell attack.)

**Physical Cohesion (3rd level).** **Freischaltung:** Stufe 3. When making a Constitution saving throw to maintain concentration on a spell while under physical duress, you can use your Strength or Dexterity modifier instead of your Constitution modifier.

### Arcane Scout (Stalker)

*You rely on mobility, stealth, and finely-honed magical senses.*

**Sensory Sweep (1st level).** **Freischaltung:** Stufe 1. You gain proficiency in Perception and Investigation. You can expend 1 mana die as an action to sense the presence of active magic, magical traps, or hidden entities within 18 meters for 10 minutes.

**Flicker Step (2nd level).** **Freischaltung:** Stufe 2. Immediately after you cast a spell of Tier 1 or higher, you can teleport up to 4.5 meters to an unoccupied space you can see. This movement does not provoke opportunity attacks.

**Surgical Magic (3rd level).** **Freischaltung:** Stufe 3. If you hit a creature that is surprised or hasn't taken a turn in the current combat with a spell, you can roll the spell's damage dice twice and use the higher result.

## Overcasting (6th level)

**Freischaltung:** Stufe 6
When you reach a Deep Yellow core (6th level), you learn to push your mana core beyond its natural limit.

Once per short or long rest, you can attempt to overcast a spell of one tier higher than your normal maximum. The cost is **double the base mana dice** (enhancement dice are not doubled). You must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC = 10 + the spell's tier) or the spell fizzles, dealing psychic damage equal to the number of dice spent — and the dice are still lost.

---

## Academic Specialization (6th level)

**Freischaltung:** Stufe 6
Beginning at 6th level, you declare a formal academic specialization. This represents your entrance into the advanced curriculum of the White Griffon Academy.

Choose one specialization from the approved list. You gain the **Apprentice** tier feature of that specialization and access to its Tier 1 and Tier 2 formulas in your formula tome.

### Deepening Your Knowledge

**Freischaltung:** Stufe 10
At **10th level** (Green core) you can either:

- **Focus on one specialization.** You improve your existing specialization to the **Practitioner** tier, gaining its Practitioner feature and unlocking Tier 3 and Tier 4 formulas. (Tier 4 formulas require Mage Association licensing — see below.)
- **Train a second specialization.** During your next Major Break you devote all your downtime to intensive study. You suffer one level of **Mental Fatigue** (see below) but gain the **Apprentice** tier of a second specialization, unlocking its Tier 1–2 formulas.

If you are a **dual specialist**, you can improve one of your specializations to Practitioner at **15th level** by again sacrificing a Major Break and suffering Mental Fatigue. You may never achieve the **Master** tier in any specialization, and you cannot unlock Tier 5+ formulas without Master status.

At **15th level** (Deep Blue core), a **single-specialization** character who has remained focused unlocks the **Master** tier of their chosen field, gaining the Master capstone feature and full access to Tier 5+ formulas.

### Mental Fatigue

A character suffering Mental Fatigue has disadvantage on initiative rolls and Wisdom (Perception) checks until they take a full Major Break off from study. The condition can also be removed by a *greater restoration* spell or similar effect.

### Dabbling

Even if you are not fully specialized, you can acquire a handful of Tier 1–2 formulas from any specialization by spending gold and study time. You gain no special features from these dabbled spells — only the ability to prepare and cast them.

---

## Mage Association Licensing (10th level+)

All Tier 4 and higher formulas are tightly controlled by the Griffon Kingdom's Mage Association. To learn a Tier 4 spell you must be a licensed Practitioner in the relevant specialization and have earned the necessary **Association Contribution Points** through military service, research, or other merits. The GM will detail specific licensing requirements during play.

---

## Ability Score Increase

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

> [!warning]- GM Only — Awakening & True Magic
>
> A Mage who undergoes genuine Awakening is forever changed. The awakening process removes the dampening impurities from the core, allowing the mage to draw world energy directly and cast without any external aids. This section should only be revealed to a player whose character has successfully awakened.
>
> **True Magic**
> - An awakened mage **ignores all verbal, somatic, and material components** for all spells. Casting becomes completely silent and motionless unless the mage chooses to display it.
> - An awakened mage can **feel** the flow of mana around them. They gain advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks made to detect magical auras, traps, or hidden spellcasters.
>
> **Spirit Magic (Tier 6)**
> - Awakened mages gain access to Spirit Magic — the element of life and mana itself. You may now learn and cast Tier 6 Spirit Magic formulas (subject to the usual licensing and mastery rules).
> - Spirit Magic spells are cast mentally at instant speed and bypass many conventional protective arrays.
>
> **Breakthroughs**
> - Awakened cores no longer grow passively. Instead they must be cultivated through extreme physical and magical exertion. When the core is ready to evolve, a **breakthrough** occurs — a violent, paralyzing internal reconstruction. The character falls prone and is helpless for several minutes while their body is rebuilt. The GM handles these as dramatic, surprise events.
> - Unlike the safe progress of a Mage, a failed breakthrough can rupture the core, causing permanent ability damage or death.
>
> **Extended Lifespan**
> - An awakened human will naturally live for around 300 years. Other races may see proportional increases.
